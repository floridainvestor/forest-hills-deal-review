Risk Flags + Negotiation + Decision Gates

Open Items · Pre-Offer Checklist · Closing Script
Indoor Pool — Decision Required Before Offer
Operational pool: adds $75/mo insurance rider, maintenance, fencing liability. Pool sealed/filled ($5–15K): eliminates liability, may free ~500+ sqft for potential 10th room. GC must assess removal or repurposing cost. Pool disposition decision directly affects insurance, room count, and NOI by $75–125/mo.
32208 Co-Living Rates — Confirm Before Offer
Model uses $160–165/wk. At $150/wk (floor), CoC drops to 17.2% at 4% IO — below the 20% floor. Call Emanuel Premate (904) 684-7482 and request 32208 weekly rates, occupancy, and fill time. If 32208 confirms below $155/wk, renegotiate IO rate to 3% to compensate.
1950 Build — Full Systems Audit
1950 construction carries real risk: cast-iron or galvanized plumbing (rust, leaks), knob-and-tube or fuse-box electrical (code violations, insurance issues), original HVAC (expect replacement), potential asbestos in insulation or flooring. GC must specifically audit all systems. $150K worst-case rehab reflects this. If GC comes in over $100K, renegotiate price or IO rate before signing.
COJ Zoning — 32208 Rooming House Permit
32208 zoning for co-living / rooming house use must be confirmed with City of Jacksonville before any offer is signed. A CUP or special use permit in 32208 could add 60–90 days and $1,500–5,000. If zoning is incompatible, this deal does not exist. Confirm at coj.net before engaging Raj further.
2nd Floor Bath Ratio — 4:1 Flag
2nd floor has 1 bath (10'11"×15'0") serving 4 proposed rooms = 4:1 ratio. Standard co-living max: 3 per bath. Options: (A) Add 2nd bath upstairs in rehab budget (~$6–10K); (B) Drop to 3 rooms on 2nd floor = 8 total; (C) Accept 4:1 and price rooms 6–9 slightly lower. Confirm bath location and decision on GC walkthrough.
ARV Unknown — DealMachine Pull Required
5,775 sqft in 32208 — comparable comps will be rare. Without ARV, Year 5 balloon exit is undefined. Minimum ARV for DSCR refi: $507K. If ARV is lower, Jerel needs an extension option or a sale exit strategy. Pull DealMachine ARV before finalizing offer price.
Electric Bill — Validate Before Close
$1,100/mo electric is the modeled figure for a 5,775 sqft, 1950-built, inefficient structure. Validate with actual seller bills before close. If bills run materially higher, revise model and adjust offer price or IO rate accordingly. Frame as professional underwriting diligence — Raj respects this.
Bundle with Skycrest — Strongest Play
Raj Mody owns both Forest Hills and Skycrest. A bundled offer on both properties simultaneously creates negotiating pressure to lower rates on both deals. Present: "I'll take both — same IO structure, two notes, you move two assets at once." This is the highest-ROMA move before any individual negotiation starts.

Negotiation Script — Raj Mody · Forest Hills (Bundle Play)

1
Open with the Bundle — Don't Negotiate Individually
"Raj, I want to make this simple for you. I'll take both properties — Forest Hills and Skycrest. Same IO structure on both. You're carrying two notes, getting reliable monthly income on two assets that have been difficult to move. I handle the rehab and operations on both."
Don't let him separate the deals. The bundle is your leverage. If he tries to do them one at a time, slow down.
2
Name the Rate You Want
"On the rate — I'm looking at 3% IO on both. Forest Hills is a 1950 build with a pool and needs significant rehab. At 3%, my numbers work and I can commit to both. At 5%, I'm stretched thin on Forest Hills specifically given the age of the building."
Let the 1950 build and pool do work for you. It's a legitimate reason to push toward 3%.
3
Pool — Put It on the Table Early
"One thing I need to address before I finalize Forest Hills — the indoor pool. I need a GC walkthrough to understand if it stays operational, gets sealed, or gets converted to usable space. That directly affects my rehab budget and my offer. Can I schedule a walk before we lock in terms?"
This buys you time and signals you're a serious, professional buyer. It also protects you from overcommitting.
4
Request Utility Bills
"Can you share the last 12 months of electric bills for Forest Hills? At this size house with a pool, I want to model my operating costs accurately. It helps me commit to a structure you can count on long-term."
Frame it as doing him a favor — better underwriting = more reliable borrower. Don't make it adversarial.
5
Close on the Extension + No Cross-Default
"Two things I want to build into both notes: a 5-year extension option at balloon, and no cross-default clause between the two properties. Each property stands on its own. That protects both of us."
Non-negotiable for Forest Hills specifically — 1950 rehab complexity means you may need the extension if something runs long. Get it in writing at signing.
